The Harsh Reality

Why Standards Alone are Not Enough

Beneath the world-class facade, thousands of global citizens face a frustratingly different reality every single day.

No English Support

  • Staff avoid calls; instructions & alerts are only in Korean.
  • Sensitive medical questions receive no real explanation.
  • Announcements during exams are in Korean only.

Confusing Process

  • Prep items arrive with no English explanation on usage.
  • Colonoscopy prep instructions in Korean often lead to failed exams.
  • Complex pre-checkup questionnaires are entirely in Korean.

Unreliable Scheduling

  • Reservation takes 2-3 days; different staff give varying answers.
  • Overseas timing/number issues block prep instructions and alerts.
  • No accountability for delayed bookings or missed calls.

Admin Chaos

  • Results mailed to unavailable addresses or delayed for weeks.
  • Insurance receipts blocked by phone verification limits.
  • Zero post-exam guidance for overseas travelers.

These issues look small on paper.
But here's what they actually look like in real life.

Before I came to Korea, everything sounded reassuring. I was told there was English support, that foreigners used the service often, and that the whole process would be smooth and well-guided.

Then I tried to make a reservation.

↓

That's when things began to fall apart.

No one could actually speak English. Every guide, alert, and preparation note was only in Korean — which was surprising, considering what had been promised.

It took days just to confirm a schedule. Calls went unanswered. Replies came late or didn't come at all. Each follow-up made me feel like I was asking for too much... even though I was only trying to understand the service.

•••

On the day of the exam, the confusion peaked. Even though they said someone would guide me, I didn't know where to go, what to bring, or who to ask.

Staff spoke quickly in Korean, pointed vaguely and moved on. One test was stopped halfway with no explanation — just a brief gesture telling me to head somewhere else.

↓

At that moment, I realized I was completely on my own.

I left Korea without receiving my results. Days turned into weeks. No follow-up. No clear answer. No one who felt responsible.

And in the end, I understood something important — the promises I heard before coming did not match the reality I faced once I arrived.

•••

It sounds unbelievable.
But this is everyday reality for foreigners in Korea.

"You might think it happens to only one unlucky person."

But in Korea, almost every foreigner becomes that "one unlucky person."
